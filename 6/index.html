Two bad options, one broken table
Imports (Steelite, RAK, Villeroy & Boch) look right and last — but carry duty, container MOQs, long lead times and get discontinued. Local unbranded Khurja is cheap and fast but batch-inconsistent, so re-orders never match. Within 12–24 months every premium venue is serving on a chipped, mismatched table.

Reading the map.

Imports like Steelite, RAK & V&B own the top on price and prestige — but are agent-distributed, duty-loaded and slow to re-supply. Indian makers (Clay Craft, Bharat Potteries, Feather Touch) have scale and 5-star clients but present as factories, not branded service. Ariane is closest — yet still hasn't fused kiln-cost, import-grade design and a held-SKU guarantee.

The top-right — premium and serviced — is uncontested. That is the investable idea.

Chapter 6 · The Annuity

The most important ₹ in the deck.

Take one 80-cover Mumbai fine-diner. The opening fit-out is a one-time ~₹7.8 lakh order. But at ~15%/yr breakage, the replacement “blades” exceed that opening order within ~6–7 years — and over a 10-year account life the annuity is bigger than the acquisition sale.

Remove the held-SKU guarantee and this collapses to a one-time commodity sale. The moat and the annuity are the same fact viewed twice: whoever holds your exact SKU and re-supplies it plate-for-plate captures the stream by default — because re-ordering from anyone else re-introduces the mismatch problem.
