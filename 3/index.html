Chapter 1 · The Opening

One idea, four numbers.

India is ageing fastest at the top of the pyramid — and the families best able to pay for care are the least able, structurally, to give it. Eight organised incumbents already compete for that household, yet every one shows the same fault line: caregiver quality. That gap is the whole thesis.

Reading the map.

Reach-first players like Samarth (350+ cities) and HCAH (70+) trade density for footprint. Portea sits highest on caregiver quality — but it owns acute medical episodes, not the ongoing relationship. Every one carries a caregiver-welfare or consistency crack in its own public data.

The top-right — deep Mumbai density and a directly-employed, published-welfare bench — is uncontested. That is the investable idea.

Chapter 6 · The Caregiver Number

The most important % in the deck.

The moat isn't the app — it's how the caregiver is treated. The whole product is trust, delivered by one human at the bedside. Yet the sector's own employer data shows caregiver welfare swings wildly — and it's invisible to the customer who's paying.

Same category, a 31-point gap in how caregivers rate their own employer — proof the bench, not the brand, is the variable. No incumbent publishes retention or tenure to customers today.
