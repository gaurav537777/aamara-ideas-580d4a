Reading the map.

Every mandate that worked — UK, Dubai, CPWD — rides a permit or procurement moment on new construction, with the owner paying.

The pitch — taxpayer funds a twin of everything — is the one corner that never gets funded. The wedge is the opposite corner.

Chapter 6 · The Money

A mandate no one funds vs a fee owners already pay.

The whole business turns on this one comparison. One bar is a wish; the other is money already changing hands, every year, that we can attach to.

Owners of malls, hospitals and high-rises already pay consultants and re-inspection fees every Fire-NOC cycle. The twin doesn't ask for a new budget — it upgrades a budget that already recurs, and adds an insurer discount on top. EST.

The government's role is to recognise the twin as the compliance artefact — like Dubai. The owner funds it because the owner keeps the value.
