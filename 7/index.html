Reading the map.

The scaled leader BookMyBai has a guarantee but is downmarket and reputationally damaged. EliteButlers is the only true premium incumbent — but publishes no managed retainer or replacement SLA. The gig wave (Snabbit, Pronto, Urban Company) is cheap, fast and high-frequency — the opposite end.

The top-right — premium and actively-managed — is uncontested. That is the investable idea.

Chapter 6 · The Human Number

The most important ₹ in the deck.

The moat isn’t marketing — it’s a career ladder. Today’s informal live-in maid earns ₹16–20k in cash with no PF, no security, no path. Formal, certified employment (maid → cook → estate manager) can reach ₹1 lakh+ — worth more inside the system than outside it. That ladder is both the ethics and the anti-poaching lock.

The workforce is overwhelmingly female, migrant, Dalit, Adivasi and Muslim, and 90%+ informal. Registered employment — PF, benefits, academy certification, a real path — beats informal cash on the one thing a good cook or estate manager most lacks: stability. That switching cost is what keeps trained staff exclusive to you, and it’s a genuine family-office ESG story rather than a bolt-on one.
